Metformin HCL 500mg

Class: Biguanides (Antidiabetic agents)

Product Identification

visibility
Form: Tablet Route: Oral

Chemical Structure

C4H11N5·HCl

science Mechanism of Action

Metformin decreases hepatic glucose production, decreases intestinal absorption of glucose, and improves insulin sensitivity by increasing peripheral glucose uptake and utilization. Unlike sulfonylureas, metformin does not produce hypoglycemia in either patients with type 2 diabetes or normal subjects and does not cause hyperinsulinemia. With metformin therapy, insulin secretion remains unchanged while fasting insulin levels and day-long plasma insulin response may actually decrease.
