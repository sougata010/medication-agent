Atorvastatin 40mg

Class: Statins (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors)

Product Identification

visibility
Form: Tablet Route: Oral

Chemical Structure

C33H35FN2O5

science Mechanism of Action

Atorvastatin is a selective, competitive inhibitor of HMG-CoA reductase, the rate-limiting enzyme responsible for the conversion of 3-hydroxy-3-methylglutaryl-coenzyme A to mevalonate, a precursor of sterols, including cholesterol. In vivo, atorvastatin primarily targets the liver, where it reduces cholesterol synthesis, subsequently leading to an upregulation of LDL receptors on the hepatocyte surface. This physiological adaptation increases the uptake and catabolism of LDL cholesterol from the systemic circulation.

warning

Interaction Analysis

Strong CYP3A4 Inhibitors High Risk

Clarithromycin, Itraconazole significantly increase atorvastatin plasma levels. Risk of myopathy/rhabdomyolysis.

Cyclosporine Modify Dose

Concomitant use requires dose limitation. Atorvastatin dose should not exceed 10 mg/day.
